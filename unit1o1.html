1.4- Describe the risks that might impact digital projects

Lack of cross-functional expertise: Like number 1, there is a common misconception that only "tech people" are required for digital projects.
But each digital project should involve someone with business process management experience to help scope and schedule the coordination between the customer journey and organisational back-end processes.
Business process misalignment: Marketing will produce a beautiful new website with a fantastic user interface, but the project is very likely to fail if they ignore the resulting effects on the business processes of the organisation.
. This also occurs when teams place technology ahead of policy, before understanding the business process ramifications of the latest technology, falling for the hype of fresh revolutionary tools.
To project performance, it is important to match digital and company priorities and incorporate new products seamlessly with business operations.
Lack of vision: technology advances rapidly, it is important to look beyond the currently available technologies and explore and invest in new and emerging technologies while embarking on digital transformation.
In order to avoid risks in the digital project you should consider increasing usability in web design. Website sites are constantly interchanging you have to comply with latest standards to build a successful website.
Furthermore it’s essential to assess unnecessary HTML codes as it could be slowing the process of the site and lower your ranking on google.(Richard Adams-Jan 29, 2015)

Under GDPR standards, the conditions for obtaining consent are stricter as the applicant must have the right to revoke consent at any time and there is a presumption that consent will not be valid unless separate consents for various processing activities are obtained.
This means that, for example, to obtain a newsletter, you have to be able to show that the person agreed to a certain action.
